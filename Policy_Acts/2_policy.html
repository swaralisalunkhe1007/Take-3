National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy

National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy (NDSAP) Overview The Government of India and various State Governments generate and manage vast amounts of data in different formats to fulfill administrative, scientific, and technical requirements. A significant portion of this data is produced by scientific institutions using substantial public funding. However, the absence of standardized formats has made data integration and interoperability a major challenge. Need for Data Sharing Global experiences have clearly shown that open access to data encourages innovation, scientific advancement, economic growth, and public welfare. Open data also strengthens civil society by enabling transparency, research, and informed decision-making. Considering the large public investment involved and the untapped potential of these datasets, it has become essential to make non-sensitive government data accessible for legitimate and registered use. Policy Background In line with the Governmentâ€™s focus on citizen participation in governance, transparency, and accountability, and in accordance with the Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005, the National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy (NDSAP) was officially notified in March 2012. The policy promotes proactive disclosure of non-strategic and non-sensitive data in the public domain. Objectives of NDSAP The National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy aims to: Improve accessibility of non-sensitive government data Enable easier data sharing among registered users Support scientific research, economic planning, and social development Enhance transparency and accountability in public administration Implementation and Data Availability Over the years, several data-holding organizations at the Central and State levels have made their non-sensitive datasets publicly available. These efforts follow the broad guidelines laid down under the RTI Act, 2005, ensuring responsible and lawful data dissemination. Nodal Department The Department of Science & Technology (DST) serves as the nodal department responsible for the coordination, formulation, implementation, and monitoring of the National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy. Geospatial Data Management For matters related to geospatial data, the existing National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI) framework is used. This mechanism involves both the Department of Space and the Department of Science & Technology and is responsible for coordination and conflict resolution related to spatial data. Gazette Notification The National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy (NDSAP) was formally notified through a Gazette Notification in March 2012, establishing a structured framework for open data governance in India.
